## 5) Generation Samples

> **Note on Accuracy:** Since this is a 146M parameter **base model** (trained primarily to predict the next token on web data, without instruction-tuning), it does not yet have the capability to follow instructions or answer questions accurately. Instead, it treats the prompt as the beginning of a document and attempts to complete it. As a result, its outputs mimic the *structure* of textbooks, code, and web forums, but remain completely hallucinated and factually inaccurate.

Here are 3 samples generated by the model at step 30,000, illustrating its current behavior:

### Sample 1: The Most Coherent Prose (Creative Writing)

**Prompt:** `Create a one-paragraph summary of a fictional chapter where a traveler discovers a hidden observatory.`
**Completion:**

```text
Create a one-paragraph summary of a fictional chapter where a traveler discovers a hidden observatory.
The story of the narrator is used to create an exploration of life in the story, which takes place between an unknown and one that encounters a historical tale, enabling you to explore how it relates with the narrative’s identity, as well as what happened after the adventurous narration. In this series, we’ll begin by describing the two stories about a story that are missing from a real or mysterious, known as the “story” story, where the story begins.
```

*(Note: While the model ignores the prompt's instructions about a traveler, it successfully produces a relatively coherent, grammatically accurate English paragraph about a story narrative, proving it has learned language structures).*
